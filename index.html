Common Questions
What you might be wondering
Do I need to be in college to join? +
Impact Lab is designed for students who are entering college or currently enrolled. If you're in your final year of secondary school and heading to university, you're also a great fit. We accept applications on a rolling basis subject to cohort availability.
How much time does it require each week? +
Expect to invest 6–8 hours per week. This includes live workshop sessions (typically 2–3 sessions per week), self-directed assignments, coaching check-ins, and community engagement. The programme is designed to be rigorous but manageable alongside your studies.
Is the programme delivered online or in person? +
The 2026 cohort is delivered in a hybrid format — live online sessions with optional in-person intensives at key milestones (onboarding, mid-point, and graduation). You'll need a reliable internet connection and a device to participate fully.
What deliverables will I actually produce? +
By graduation you'll have produced: a Personal Mission Statement, a Personal Development Plan (PDP), a professional CV, a LinkedIn profile, a digital portfolio, a simple business plan and pitch, a personal budget template with a 90-day finance plan, and a personal wellness plan. These aren't just exercises — they're tools you carry into real life.
Can I get a refund if the programme isn't right for me? +
We offer a full refund within the first 7 days of the programme if you decide it's not the right fit. After that, we do not offer refunds but you may defer to the next cohort (subject to availability and a deferral fee of $30).
What if I can't afford the full fee? +
We offer the monthly payment plan for exactly this reason. We also have a limited number of partial scholarships available for students with demonstrated financial need — reach out to us directly at admissions@impactlab.co to inquire before applying.
